History of cats

.

In ancient Egypt, cats were loved for their ability to protect homes from pests and dangerous animals like snakes and scorpions. They also held a deep religious significance, par ticularly through the goddess Bastet. Egyptians mummified cats and dedicated them to the goddess, even placing them in tombs to accompany their owners into the afterlife. This reverence was so strong that killing a cat, even accidentally, could result in severe punishment, and cats were cherished companions, sometimes adorned with jewelry.

Egyptian Myths

Egyptian myths and legends centered on the cat Bastet, who transformed from a fierce lioness into a gentle protector of home, family, and fertility, as well as a fierce warrior who battled evil. The belief in cats' divine energy was so strong that harming one was a capital offense, leading to their mummification and dedicated burial sites. Cats were seen as sacred, with legends even claiming they possessed divine abilities, such as returning from fires and having "nine lives".
